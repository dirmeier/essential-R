3 Data wrangling

Every analysis of data starts with preprocessing and parsing. For this Iâ€™ve found dplyr and tidyr tremendously useful. There are many more great tools in the tidyverse and Rstudio, such as purrr (explained later), magrittr or tibble, but these two simlify most of my work.

library(dplyr)
data(iris)
group_by(iris, Species) %>% summarize(Petal.Length = mean(Petal.Length), Sepal.Length = mean(Sepal.Length)) %>% 
    mutate(Species = toupper(Species))
